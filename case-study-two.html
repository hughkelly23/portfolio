UX / Product Design

Roost.

Mobile app concept · Roast dinner planning · 2026

A calm cooking companion that helps users plan, coordinate, and time every part of a roast dinner without the usual last-minute stress.

Problem

Coordinating roast timings across multiple dishes and appliances.

Audience

UK-based beginner to intermediate home cooks aged 25–70.

Goal

Reduce cooking stress and increase confidence.

Role

UX Research, Branding, UX/UI Design

01 / Product

Product Description

Roost is a mobile app concept designed to help people cook a full roast dinner by working backwards from the time they want to serve. Instead of forcing users to manually calculate when each dish needs to start, Roost creates a coordinated cooking plan with clear timings, appliance choices, and step-by-step guidance.

Core Problem

Roast dinners are stressful because several elements need to finish at the same time. Users need support with timings, sequencing, pressure, and confidence.

Minimum Viable Product

Users enter their serving time, choose a roast, select sides and appliances, then receive a timed cooking schedule with in-app reminders.

Project Goals

Reduce stress, improve confidence, simplify coordination, and support independent cooking through contextual guidance.

02 / Journey

Customer Journey

The journey maps how a user might discover Roost, consider whether it solves their problem, complete their first roast plan, return for future meals, and eventually recommend it to others.

03 / Research

Competitor Analysis

Competitor analysis explored recipe apps and roast-timing tools to understand where Roost could sit in the market. The opportunity was to combine the clarity of a timing tool with the polish and guidance of a modern cooking app.

Market Gap

Existing products either focus on broad recipe discovery or simple roast timers. Roost sits between both: a guided orchestration tool for a specific, high-pressure meal.

Opportunity

Create a focused app that reduces cognitive load during stressful cooking moments through clear sequencing, failsafe reminders, and appliance flexibility.

04 / Users

Personas

Two working personas helped frame Roost around different levels of cooking confidence and digital confidence.

Jamie Carter

Beginner student cook

Jamie is comfortable with technology but lacks cooking confidence. He wants simple step-by-step guidance and finds full roast dinners stressful to coordinate.

Liz Foster

Intermediate home cook

Liz cooks regularly but prefers clear written instructions, readable layouts, and simple interfaces. She wants support without unnecessary complexity.
